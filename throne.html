Overview

Project Throne is a name for our mobile application that began the process of development during the 2019 UWB Hacks the Internet event. While still in the early stages of design, the idea for this application is a reflection of what we, as university students, would find very helpful to us if made into a reality.

Objective

The goal of this project is to create a product that will help university of washington students find a restroom on campus of their liking. It will allow students to search and filter out restrooms on campus of their desired preference, and should include a way for students to help navigate to their desired restroom.

Approach

After settling with a concrete idea, we needed to figure out what form will our product take place on. We decided with a mobile application since it would be the most convenient for university students to use and search for restrooms around campus. The next steps would include research, designing, and prototyping. The research for this application will be the most time-consuming as we will need to utilize the University of Washington's mapping plan for every building to figure out the locations of restrooms. We will then need to spend our time looking through each restroom and taking note of its features (such as whether paper towels are available, or the number of stalls). From there we will implement the information in our wireframe and start early testing of our application and going through iterations to see what works and what doesn't.
